About

I am currently studying biostatistics at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health. Prior to entering this program, I worked for 5 years as a research professional under Dr. Scott Sponheim at the Cognition & Brain (CAB) lab at the University of Minnesota, Twin-cities. During my time there, I worked on a longitudinal study devoted to using magnetoencephalography (MEG) and functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) to examine the neural mechanisms that underlie the visual anomalies, such as hallucinations, in people with psychosis. My primary responsibilities included data pre-processing and analysis, as well as data collection and maintenance. Additional information on my professional experiences can be found on my Linkedin page.
